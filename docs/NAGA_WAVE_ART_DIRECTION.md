## THE WAVE'S VISUAL THESIS — "VENOM IS LIGHT IN MOTION"
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
The launch Naga set painted serpents and poison as PRESENCE: coiled
kings, fanged strikes, venom as a property of a creature. The wave's
design theme is the CORPSE ECONOMY and TOKEN MASTERY — venom that
moves, transfers, accumulates, converts. So the wave's unifying
visual motif is venom rendered as THIN LUMINOUS GREEN LIGHT IN
MOTION, and the direction of that motion IS the card's mechanic:

- flowing OUT of a figure  → Kulika (he transfers it away)
- flowing INTO an object   → Throne of the Second King (it steals)
- threaded BETWEEN targets → Shankhapala (it moves tokens)
- drawn IN from the air    → Venom Harvester (it profits)
- ESCAPING at death        → Molting Naga (death delivers)
- absorbed FROM the fallen → Vishalakshi (she grows on venom deaths)
- carried ON the self      → Uraga Colossus (the sanctioned price)

A player who has felt the card should recognize the art before
reading the name. When you compose or reroll, protect the motion
direction above everything else — it is the card's identity.

## GLOBAL LAWS (every card)
1. PATALA PALETTE LAW: deep emerald-teal on obsidian black. One
   permitted accent per card (noted per card). Everything else
   desaturated.
2. VEINS, NOT LAMPS: bioluminescence is thin lines and threads —
   the moment green light becomes a glow-source filling the frame,
   the card frame loses. The ART must stay darker and cooler than
   the teal border; anything brighter than the frame's gold text is
   the enemy (the board-art law, applied to cards).
3. FRAME SAFE-AREAS (2:3 portrait under the Canva template): the
   focal subject lives in the upper-central 60%. The bottom third
   belongs to the text plate — atmosphere only, no critical detail.
   Outer 8% on all sides is border territory. Eyes/focal points sit
   at roughly the upper-third line.
4. NO SCRIPT EVER: MJ invents gibberish glyphs on thrones, altars,
   and ritual floors — ban lettering in every generation and veto
   any pick where carvings read as writing.
5. MENACE REGISTER: patient, luxurious, inevitable — never gore,
   never horror. Serpents threaten through scale, stillness, and
   certainty. Deities and royals keep the established respectful
   card register.
6. RAWS ARE ASSETS: save every pre-Canva pick — they are the sref
   pool for cutscenes, the trailer, and future alt-art.

### 1. KULIKA — Hero · P8 · Legendary
FILE: Nagas_Hero_Kulika_P8_rLegendary.png
TXT: ON PLAY: Transfer all Venom from your Units to random enemy
Units.
WHO: one of the eight nagarajas — a true serpent KING, not a
monster. The vault's masculine crown piece opposite Padmavati.
THE MECHANIC IN THE FRAME: he is the great UNBURDENER — venom
leaves him and his kin. Luminous green ribbons stream OUT of his
coils and away into darkness, leaving his own scales clean and
black. The motion direction (outward, away) is non-negotiable.
COMPOSITION: single colossal crowned cobra rising vertically from
black water — the vertical serpent fills the 2:3 frame naturally.
Hood open at the upper third; crown (obsidian, one dim green gem)
at the focal point. The out-flowing venom ribbons curve down and
outward toward the lower corners, leading the eye out of frame.
LIGHT: the ribbons are the only light; his scales catch their edge
as thin rim-light. Water black, matte.
MOOD: regal, unhurried, generous in the way only the powerful are.
GALLERY CONTEXT: sits beside KALIYA — must not echo him. Kaliya's
launch art owns the multi-head fan and the river; Kulika is ONE
hood, ONE crown, subterranean water. Also beside Vasuki/Shesha:
his crown must be lesser than theirs (a king, not THE king).
AVOID: multiple heads · river/surface water · daylight · venom
flowing inward (inverts the card).
Optional prompt: crowned single-hooded colossal cobra rising from
black subterranean water, streams of luminous green venom flowing
out of his coils away into darkness, obsidian scales with thin
emerald rim-light, regal and unhurried, painterly, --ar 2:3.

### 2. NAHUSHA, FALLEN KING — Unit · P6 · Epic
FILE: Nagas_Unit_NahushaFallenKing_P6_rEpic.png

WHO: the myth IS the card — the mortal king who sat on Indra's

THE MECHANIC IN THE FRAME: a king claiming what isn't his, mid-

LIGHT: one faint cold light from above (the realm he stole looking
down at him); teal veins climbing the serpent half only.
MOOD: tragic grandeur. The most HUMAN image in the wave.
GALLERY CONTEXT: two thrones exist in this faction's art (launch
Patala Throne, wave Second King). Nahusha's seat must read as a
RUINED MORTAL throne — cracked stone, wrong scale for him — not an
obsidian serpent-throne. Three thrones, three registers.
AVOID: fully human body · fully serpent body (the between-state is
the card) · bright throne room · triumphant posture.
Optional prompt: fallen king on a cracked stone throne, lower body

### 3. THRONE OF THE SECOND KING — Artifact · Mythic
FILE: Nagas_Artifact_ThroneoftheSecondKing_rMythic.png

WHAT: the wave's crown jewel and the vault's centerpiece — the

THE MECHANIC IN THE FRAME: theft rendered as drinking. Thin

throne mass in the upper-central 60%.
LIGHT: the floor-streams are the light source, lighting the throne

MOOD: oppressive patience. Furniture that outlasted its kings.
GALLERY CONTEXT: must not echo the launch Patala Throne frame

AVOID: any seated figure · skulls (horror register) · bright gems

Optional prompt: empty obsidian throne carved from a single coiled
