## Tab 5 (shown FIRST): HOW TO WIN

**Header:** How to win
**Tagline:** Before you pick an army, learn the one rule every army obeys.

**Body copy:**
Divya Yuddha is best of three rounds — but your cards must last all three. Every card
you spend winning a round is a card you cannot spend later. The player who wins two
rounds wins the match; the player who tries to win all three usually loses.

**Diagram U1 — "The Round Economy" (3-panel strip):**
- Panel 1: ROUND 1 — two hand-size bars: YOU 8 cards (spend 2, concede), ENEMY 8 cards
  (spends 5, wins round). Caption: "Lose the round. Keep your cards."
- Panel 2: ROUND 2 — YOU 6 cards vs ENEMY 3 cards. Caption: "Now you outnumber them."
- Panel 3: ROUND 3 — YOU win both remaining rounds on card advantage. Caption:
  "Two rounds win the match. The third was a gift you gave them."

**Diagram U2 — "When to Pass" (3-panel strip):**
- Panel 1: You are AHEAD on board and the enemy passes. Caption: "They passed while
  behind? Play one more card, then pass. Never two."
- Panel 2: You are BEHIND by more than your best card. Caption: "Concede. A round lost
  by 20 costs the same as a round lost by 1."
- Panel 3: FINAL ROUND. Caption: "There is no next round to save for. Spend everything."

**Callout box:** Mantras are limited to 2 per deck and Artifacts to 2 per deck with one
active at a time. These are your match-deciding plays — spend them in the round you
intend to win, not the round you happen to be losing.

## Tab 1: DEVAS

**Tagline:** The shining ones. They do not fall — and if they fall, they rise.
**Difficulty:** Beginner · **Playstyle:** Protection and endurance
**Mechanic box — DHARMA SHIELD:** Deva play revolves around protecting what matters.
Dharma Shields stop enemy Astras from touching your key units, and Deva revival effects
bring the fallen back. Your units are individually strong; keep them alive and the math
wins itself.

**Strengths:**
- Toughest units to remove
- Revival and cleansing (Gayatri Mantra, Pavamana)
- Punishes Astra-heavy enemies (Indra's Net)
- Most forgiving faction to learn

**Weaknesses:**
- Slow poison loses to no poison: Nagas grind through shields (the Deva–Naga matchup
  is the hardest counter in the game — expect to fight uphill)
- Fewer explosive plays; you win by margins, not spikes
- Protection spent on the wrong unit is protection wasted

**Diagram D1 — "The Unbreakable Wall":**
Step 1: Amrita Kalasha early [card thumb] — "Your weakest unit grows every round and
revives if killed."
Step 2: Dharma Shield your strongest unit [shield icon over card thumb] — "Their removal
now has no target worth hitting."
Result: "Win the long round. Their damage bounced; yours didn't."

**Diagram D2 — "Turn Their Weapons":**
Step 1: Indra's Net on board [card thumb].
Step 2: Enemy plays an Astra [enemy card, crossed arrow] — "Every enemy Astra feeds a
random Deva +2."
Result: "Astra decks must choose: hold their weapons, or arm yours."

## Tab 2: ASURAS

**Tagline:** The breakers of rules. Power now, price later — if later ever comes.
**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Playstyle:** Aggression and calculated risk
**Mechanic box — CHAOS SURGE:** Asura Astras can surge with bonus chaos effects. Your
weapons hit harder than anyone's — sometimes harder than you planned. Asura play is
about choosing when the price is worth paying.

**Strengths:**
- The heaviest single units in the game (Kumbhakarna, Mahabali)
- Astra amplification (Chandrahas) — your removal decides rounds
- Steal and corrupt enemy resources (Sanjivani Corruption)
- Highest mirror-tested win rate at launch

**Weaknesses:**
- Self-destructive costs: Ahamkara kills its own target at round end; Tripura shatters
  the moment any Astra is played
- Overextending into a conceded round wastes your explosiveness
- Chaos is chaos — surges can't be scheduled

**Diagram A1 — "The Doubled Blade":**
Step 1: Chandrahas on board [card thumb] — "Your first Astra each round is amplified."
Step 2: Open the round with your best Astra [card thumb + fire icon].
Result: "One play, double payload, Chaos Surge on top. Rounds end early when you start
them like this."

**Diagram A2 — "The Ego Gambit":**
Step 1: A round you MUST win [round indicator].
Step 2: Ahamkara on Kumbhakarna [card thumbs] — "Double a giant. He dies at round end
— so make it the round end that decides the match."
Result: "Pay the price in a round where the price is already refunded."

## Tab 3: VANARAS

**Tagline:** The devoted. One is weak. Ten thousand, moving as one, are not.
**Difficulty:** Easy to learn, deep to master · **Playstyle:** Momentum and swarm
**Mechanic box — LEAP:** Vanara units can Leap — copying the strength of allies already
on the board. Your army grows sideways: every unit you play makes the next one better.
Go wide, then make wide tall.

**Strengths:**
- The biggest single power spike in the game (Rama Naam with Hanuman on board)
- Leap turns one good unit into several (Kishkindha Crown doubles down)
- Buffs stack across the whole board — your total grows faster than any single removal
  can cut
- Immune-to-Naga tricks in key moments (Rama Naam cannot be countered by Naga effects;
  Rama's Signet negates Venom entirely)

**Weaknesses:**
- Individually small units — a thin board is a weak board
- Board-wide drains (Sarpa Satra) punish exactly what you want to do
- The spike is once per match: waste Rama Naam and the ceiling drops hard

**Diagram V1 — "The Rama Naam Spike":**
Step 1: Go wide — 4+ Vanaras on board [row of small card thumbs].
Step 2: Land Hanuman [card thumb] — "The army's heart arrives."
Step 3: Rama Naam [card thumb] — "+3 to every Vanara. On a full board that is the
largest single play in the game."
Result: "Save it for the round that decides the match — it only happens once."

**Diagram V2 — "Leap Economics":**
Step 1: Kishkindha Crown on board [card thumb] — "Every Leap now feeds both units, and
you may Leap twice per round."
Step 2: Leap onto your strongest unit [arrow between card thumbs].
Result: "Copies plus bonuses: one strong unit becomes an economy."

## Tab 4: NAGAS

**Tagline:** The serpent kings of Patala. They do not rush. They do not need to.
**Difficulty:** Advanced · **Playstyle:** Attrition and inevitability
**Mechanic box — VENOM:** Venom Tokens drain enemy power round after round. No single
tick matters; all of them together decide the match. You are not racing your opponent
— you are outlasting them. Your win condition is time itself.

**Strengths:**
- Inevitable chip damage that ignores unit quality
- The longer the round, the stronger you are
- Corrupted revival: even the dead serve the poison (Mrityunjaya, Ananta Coil)
- The designed counter to Devas — shields don't stop attrition

**Weaknesses:**
- Slow starts: short rounds starve the Venom clock
- Rama's Signet switches your faction mechanic off entirely
- Demands planning two rounds ahead — mistakes compound quietly

**Diagram N1 — "The Patient Kingdom":**
Step 1: Patala Throne early [card thumb] — "The drain grows every round it sits."
Step 2: Karkotaka on board [card thumb] — "Venom now strikes on every enemy turn, not
just at round's end."
Result: "Stall the round. Every turn they take costs them power. Time is on your
payroll."

**Diagram N2 — "Death Serves the Poison":**
Step 1: Ananta Coil on board [card thumb] — "Your dead leave permanent Venom behind."
Step 2: Sarpa Satra a big friendly unit [card thumb + sacrifice icon] — "Every enemy
loses its power; Venom doubles this round."
Result: "One sacrifice, board-wide drain, and a permanent token that keeps collecting.
Nagas never trade one for one."
