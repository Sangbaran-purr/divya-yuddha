## THE PALETTE LAW v2 — THE TWO-TONE RULING (codified)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Your accepted batch settled it: the wave's world is the BLUE-BLACK
ABYSS — colder and deeper than the launch set's emerald surface.
Codified as law:
- WORLD: blue-black water, blue-grey stone, cold abyssal light.
  This is "The Deep" reading deeper than the launch serpents.
- VENOM: stays GREEN, always — venom is a GAMEPLAY color and must
  read as itself against the blue world (green pops harder on blue
  than it ever did on emerald). Any luminous venom stream, token,
  drop, mist, or vein is green; everything else may be blue.
- Veins not lamps, art darker than the frame, no script — all v1
  laws stand.
- The motion thesis (venom's flow direction = the mechanic) is
  DEMOTED from law to tool: use it when composing, drop it when a
  presence portrait is stronger. Your call per card; the play
  animations carry the mechanics for the Ratna six regardless.

skulls, carvings never script.

   world? (The one legibility law that survives all taste calls —
   venom is a gameplay color.)

   the Cinzel title; carvings never script.
3. Gallery collisions: Kulika≠Kaliya · Hatchling≠launch Hatchling ·
   Second King≠Patala Throne≠Nahusha's ruin.
